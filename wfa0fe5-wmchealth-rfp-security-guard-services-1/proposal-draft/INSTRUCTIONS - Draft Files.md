# Draft File Guide

This file explains the purpose of each file in this draft proposal folder and how each one should be used during final proposal assembly.

## 00 Compliance Matrix.md

Purpose:
Tracks the required proposal components against the draft files in this folder.

Use it for:
- Checking what is complete, partial, or still placeholder content
- Tracking outstanding items before final submission
- Making sure the proposal package matches the RFP requirements

## 01 Title Page.md

Purpose:
Provides the front page of the proposal submission.

Use it for:
- Listing the RFP number and proposal title
- Inserting final company name, submission date, and any required identification details
- Creating the finished cover page when the proposal is assembled

## 02 Table of Contents.md

Purpose:
Provides the structure of the final proposal package.

Use it for:
- Listing the final sections in submission order
- Updating page numbers after the proposal is formatted into its final version
- Confirming that no required sections are missing

## 03 Transmittal Letter.md

Purpose:
Serves as the formal cover letter submitting the proposal to WMCHealth.

Use it for:
- Inserting the authorized signatory and proposal contact information
- Confirming willingness to contract under the RFP terms
- Briefly stating the proposer's intent and overall fit for the opportunity

## 04 Executive Summary.md

Purpose:
Provides the high-level summary of the proposal.

Use it for:
- Explaining the proposer's overall understanding of the engagement
- Summarizing the operating approach and key differentiators
- Giving evaluators a concise overview before they read the detailed sections

## 05 Proposer Background and Experience.md

Purpose:
Describes the company, its qualifications, leadership structure, and relevant operating experience.

Use it for:
- Presenting company history, scale, and organizational structure
- Describing experience relevant to healthcare or comparable security work
- Inserting company-specific facts, staffing numbers, office information, and financial materials

## 06 Experience and References.md

Purpose:
Provides client experience examples and reference information.

Use it for:
- Listing former and current clients as required by the RFP
- Identifying comparable healthcare or complex security engagements
- Supplying at least the required number of references with contact details

## 07 Staffing Proposal.md

Purpose:
Explains how the work will be staffed, supervised, trained, and managed.

Use it for:
- Describing the proposed management structure and staffing model
- Explaining training, supervision, and service metrics
- Adding site-by-site staffing schedules, hours, or matrices if those are included in the final submission

## 08 Additional Information.md

Purpose:
Provides extra narrative support for why the company should be selected.

Use it for:
- Highlighting differentiators that do not fit neatly elsewhere
- Explaining value-added features, transition strengths, and oversight approach
- Strengthening the best-value case for award

## 09 Conflicts of Interest and Disclosures.md

Purpose:
Captures required legal, ethical, and disclosure-related statements.

Use it for:
- Disclosing conflicts of interest
- Addressing litigation, sanctions, prior terminations, ownership concerns, or similar required declarations
- Inserting reviewed language from legal or ownership stakeholders before submission

## 10 Fee Proposal Placeholder.md

Purpose:
Marks the need for a separate fee proposal document and outlines what pricing content it must contain.

Use it for:
- Building the final pricing package in the required structure
- Separating pricing from the narrative proposal if the RFP requires separate submission
- Confirming that pricing includes entity-by-entity, position-by-position, and armed/unarmed treatment where applicable

Note:
This is not the final fee proposal. It is a placeholder and instruction point for the pricing package.

## 11 Attachments Checklist.md

Purpose:
Tracks the attachments and forms that must accompany the proposal.

Use it for:
- Confirming completion of required attachments and certifications
- Checking whether forms such as Attachments C, D, E, F, and G have been completed
- Preventing submission errors caused by missing forms

## How To Use This Folder

- Treat this folder as the working narrative package for the proposal.
- Replace placeholders with company-approved information before final assembly.
- Keep pricing, disclosures, and attachments aligned with the compliance matrix.
- Use the Q&A clarifications where they strengthen the narrative, but do not invent facts the RFP or Q&A did not confirm.
- Before submission, convert these working drafts into a final formatted proposal set with consistent headings, pagination, and attachments.

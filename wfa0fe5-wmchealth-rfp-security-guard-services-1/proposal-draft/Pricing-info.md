Pricing Guidance from Q&A

What they confirmed:

- Preferred format: Hourly rate per position, presented separately by position and by entity
  (Q136, Q185)
- Holiday coverage: Vendor provides; 1.5x rate applies for extra coverage requests with less
  than 24-hour notice (Q55)
- Training: Some training is separately billable (Q108)
- Payment terms: Net 90 days, certified payroll required with invoices (Q126)

What they refused to disclose:

- Current wage rates or billing rates (Q154)
- Annual contract spend or budget (Q96, Q97, Q189)
- Specific prevailing wage schedules — directed vendors to look them up online (Q161)

Prevailing wage note: Valhalla and MHRH are subject to NYS Prevailing Wage; BSCHS and  
 HealthAlliance are not. HealthAlliance officers are under a CBA.

So for your fee proposal, you'll need to build up fully-burdened hourly rates by position  
 using the NYS prevailing wage schedules for Valhalla/MHRH, and your own wage structure for
the other sites.

1. Wage/Rate Inputs  


- NYS Prevailing Wage rates for Westchester County — base wage + supplemental benefit rates  
  for security guard classifications at Valhalla and MHRH. Look up on the NYS DOL prevailing  
  wage portal (PRC numbers for the applicable counties).
- Your own wage scale for BSCHS, GSH, SACH — you set this, but it needs to be competitive for
  the Hudson Valley market.
- HealthAlliance CBA terms — not disclosed until award, but you need to estimate or note the
  risk.  


2. Armed vs. Unarmed Breakdown by Post  


The hours chart shows posts but doesn't always clearly identify which are armed. You know  
 24/7 armed is required at all 6 main sites, but you'll need to price armed vs. unarmed hours
separately since the RFP preferred format requires it (Q185). The Valhalla chart shows one  
 "Armed Officers" row at 168 hrs/week, but in practice more posts may be armed — this is a
pricing risk.

3. Fully Burdened Rate Components

To calculate your hourly bill rate, you need to build up:

- Payroll taxes (FICA ~7.65%, FUTA, SUTA)
- Workers' comp insurance — security guard WC rates are significant, especially armed  
  officers (typically higher mod)
- Benefits (health, dental, vision, 401k) — all vendor-provided per Q166
- PTO/vacation accrual
- Unemployment insurance
- Overhead/G&A
- Profit margin  


4. Equipment & Vehicle Costs (Amortized into Rates or Shown Separately)

- 4 vehicles (2 Valhalla, 1 MHRH, 1 HAHV): purchase/lease, insurance, fuel, maintenance,  
  light bars, decals — est. <10,000 mi/year each
- Firearms & ammo per armed officer: 9mm pistols, CCI Gold Dot 124gr hollow point, annual  
  qualification training
- Uniforms: Police-style for unarmed, blazer for armed — initial issue + replacement cycle
- Cell phones and tablets for officers
- Level III ballistic vests for armed officers (vendor-provided)

5. Insurance Costs

The required coverages are unusually high — get actual quotes for:

- $5M Cyber Liability
- $5M General Liability
- $2M Firearms Liability
- $2M Professional Liability
- $2M Auto Liability
- Fidelity/3-D bond, Umbrella

These will materially affect your bill rate, especially firearms and cyber.

6. Training Costs (Billable vs. Absorbed)  


- CPP (Crisis Prevention Program): 16hr initial, 8hr annual refresher — vendor provides  
  training and instructors
- CPR, AED — absorbed in onboarding costs
- 8hr WMCHealth orientation — some training is separately billable (Q108/Q115), but you need
  to decide what to absorb vs. bill

7. Transition/Mobilization Costs  


- Background checks, health screenings (COVID, MMR/Varicella, TB, drug screen, physical) per
  officer
- Pre-deployment hiring and onboarding period costs
- No phase-in timeline confirmed (Q57 not answered, Q182 undetermined) — price accordingly  


8. Escalation Provision  


Q187 was not answered. You should build in and propose an annual escalation mechanism (CPI or
fixed %) — especially critical given prevailing wage exposure over the contract term.

9. Overhead/Supervision

The charts show supervisory hours (Sergeant, Lieutenant, Captain, Shift Supervisor, Account  
 Manager roles), but you'll want to confirm your proposed management structure matches what's
in the charts and price any network-level account management separately.

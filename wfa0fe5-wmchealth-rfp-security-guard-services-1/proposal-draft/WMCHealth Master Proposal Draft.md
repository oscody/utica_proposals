## 2. Executive Summary

Utica Security Services Inc. submits this proposal to provide security guard services throughout the Westchester Medical Center Health Network in accordance with RFP `WFA0FE5`. We understand that WMCHealth requires a security partner capable of operating across a large, multi-campus healthcare system with differing operational needs, including acute care hospitals, behavioral health assignments, control center staffing, visitor and weapons screening, patient-related support functions, armed coverage at designated locations, and transportation-related duties such as escort, courier, and shuttle support.

Our proposed approach is built around five priorities:

1. Reliable staffing and coverage integrity across all assigned campuses and shifts
2. Healthcare-focused officer training with strong emphasis on de-escalation, customer service, emergency response, and support in high-risk patient-facing environments
3. Active supervision, documented quality assurance, and transparent reporting
4. Rapid operational responsiveness for incidents, staffing gaps, and post-order changes
5. Scalable implementation that can absorb varying site conditions, incumbent transition requirements, and WMCHealth-specific procedures

Utica Security Services Inc. proposes to provide the full range of services contemplated by Section 4 of the RFP, including concierge and administrative security support, 24/7 control center staffing, CCTV and alarm monitoring, patrol functions, behavioral health area assignments, patient screening and property safeguarding, visitor screening, clinician support during restraint-related events, escort and transportation support, armed officer coverage at designated sites, and vendor-furnished uniforms, vehicles, and required field equipment.

Our operating model is structured to support a healthcare environment where professionalism, calm decision-making, and consistent escalation discipline are critical. Officers assigned to WMCHealth would be trained to work in public-facing hospital settings, respond appropriately to behavioral escalation, follow emergency codes and panic alarm procedures, and coordinate effectively with hospital leadership, clinicians, and external emergency responders. Supervisory oversight, documented inspections, electronic recordkeeping, and corrective action management would be used to maintain service quality and compliance.

This draft proposal is based on the currently available RFP materials and assumes that certain implementation details, including final site-by-site post assignments, incumbent transition conditions, and any walkthrough findings, will be confirmed with WMCHealth during the procurement and mobilization process.

## 5. Staffing Proposal

### 5.1 Staffing Approach

Utica Security Services Inc. proposes a management and staffing model designed for a multi-campus healthcare network with varied post types, high public visibility, and a mix of preventive, responsive, and support-based security functions. The model is built to support continuous coverage, rapid management escalation, and site-specific adaptation as final post orders and transition details are confirmed.

The proposed staffing structure is intended to support:

- Lobby and concierge coverage
- Security operations/control center staffing
- CCTV, alarm, and radio monitoring
- Behavioral health area assignments
- Patient and visitor screening
- Patient property safeguarding
- Escort and transportation support
- Armed officer coverage at designated locations
- Vehicle patrols and campus roving coverage
- Courier and shuttle support where required

### 5.2 Proposed Project Team Structure

| Role | Function | Level of Effort |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Executive Sponsor | Executive oversight, contract authority, strategic escalation | As needed / on-call |
| Director of Operations | Overall performance oversight, client relationship management | Weekly review plus escalation availability |
| Security Operations Manager | Daily contract oversight, staffing coordination, reporting review | Full management responsibility |
| Site Supervisors | Shift oversight, inspections, coaching, incident escalation | Daily field presence |
| Control Center Officers | Monitoring, dispatch support, radio and alarm response | 24/7 as required |
| Armed Security Officers | Armed coverage at designated facilities | 24/7 at required sites |
| Unarmed Security Officers | Screening, concierge, patrol, and support assignments | Per finalized staffing plan |
| Relief/Floater Staff | Call-out coverage, surge response, schedule resilience | Maintained as reserve capacity |
| Compliance/Training Coordinator | Training records, onboarding, certification tracking | Ongoing support |

### 5.3 Staffing Justification

WMCHealth's scope requires a vendor with enough operational depth to support different security functions across multiple hospital environments without allowing coverage failures at one site to degrade service at another. This staffing approach is justified by the following conditions in the RFP:

- Multiple acute care locations across the network
- Continuous security operations, including 24/7 control center functions
- Specialized assignments in behavioral health areas
- Visitor and weapons screening responsibilities
- Escort and support functions involving staff, patients, and property
- Armed coverage requirements at specific hospital locations
- Need for immediate response to panic alarms, emergency codes, workplace violence, and active-threat events

For those reasons, the model includes dedicated management oversight, shift-level supervision, reserve coverage capacity, and a compliance function rather than relying solely on a scheduler and frontline officers.

### 5.4 Customer Service Training

Because WMCHealth security personnel serve in public-facing hospital environments, customer service training is treated as an operational requirement rather than a soft-skill add-on. Initial and ongoing customer-service training would address:

- Professional reception and concierge conduct
- Visitor engagement and wayfinding assistance
- Respectful communication with patients, families, clinicians, and staff
- Trauma-informed and de-escalation-oriented communication
- Maintaining calm demeanor under stress
- Communication in screening, escort, and access-control contexts
- Handling complaints and tense interactions without escalation

Customer-service expectations would be reinforced through orientation, post-order training, supervisory observation, and remedial coaching when needed.

### 5.5 Initial and Ongoing Training

Utica Security Services Inc. would implement a healthcare-oriented training program that aligns with WMCHealth requirements and site conditions. Drawing on the firm's existing training materials, the WMCHealth training program should include:

- New York State pre-assignment, on-the-job, and annual in-service training compliance
- Patrol techniques and situational awareness
- Emergency response procedures
- Active shooter preparedness and response
- First aid, CPR, AED, and overdose-response awareness as permitted by site protocols
- Observation, documentation, and incident-scene preservation
- Communication protocols for radios, alarms, dispatch, and escalation
- Handling difficult individuals and de-escalation
- Hands-on scenario-based use-of-force and defensive-control training for authorized assignments
- WMCHealth-required orientation and mandatory learning modules
- WMCHealth-approved de-escalation training with annual refresher training
- Behavioral health awareness and crisis-response principles
- Workplace violence recognition and response
- Visitor screening and weapons detection procedures
- Patient property safeguarding procedures
- Escort and transportation safety procedures

### 5.6 Quality Assurance and Performance Monitoring

The training and staffing program would be reinforced by a quality-control framework that uses documented inspections and performance-linked corrective action. Based on the firm's existing QA materials, that framework should include:

- Weekly documented site inspections during early deployment, followed by continued routine and random inspections
- Review of officer presence, punctuality, appearance, post-order compliance, and public interaction
- Monthly reporting summarizing inspections, findings, and corrective actions
- Internal monitoring of training records, licensing, scheduling compliance, and incident trends
- Immediate remediation for critical deficiencies and tracked closure of moderate and minor findings

## 6. Additional Information

Utica Security Services Inc. offers a combination of operational discipline, management responsiveness, and adaptable field execution that fits the complexity of the WMCHealth environment. WMCHealth is not seeking a commodity guard vendor. It is seeking a partner able to sustain coverage across multiple campuses while supporting control center functions, screening operations, behavioral-health-sensitive assignments, emergency response, and patient- and visitor-facing interactions. That requires more than staffing. It requires management control.

The differentiators emphasized in this draft proposal are:

- A service model built around active supervision instead of passive post filling
- Strong de-escalation and emergency-response orientation for high-stress environments
- Willingness to furnish uniforms, vehicles, field equipment, and armed-post support in line with the RFP
- Structured reporting, recordkeeping, inspections, and corrective action processes
- A practical understanding that healthcare security must balance safety, professionalism, patient dignity, and public confidence

If selected, Utica Security Services Inc. would work with WMCHealth to validate post orders, confirm site-by-site operating expectations, establish reporting cadence, and refine the transition plan around the realities of each campus. This approach is intended to reduce implementation risk while improving consistency, transparency, and service accountability from the first day of contract performance.

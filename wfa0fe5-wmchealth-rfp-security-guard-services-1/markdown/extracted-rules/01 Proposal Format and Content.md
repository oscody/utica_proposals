# Proposal Format and Content

Source: Section `2.9 Proposal Format and Content` from the WMCHealth RFP markdown.

## Core Rule

Proposals should provide a straightforward, complete, and concise description of the vendor’s capabilities to satisfy the RFP requirements. Proposals must state the assumptions made when preparing the proposal.

## Required Proposal Sections

### A. Title Page

The title page should show:

- proposed title
- name of the proposer
- proposer address
- name and title of the contact person
- name and title of the person authorized to represent the proposer
- date

### B. Table of Contents

The table of contents should clearly identify all items of the proposal by question number and page number.

### C. Transmittal Letter

The transmittal letter:

- is an integral part of the proposal
- must be signed by the individual or individuals authorized to bind the firm contractually
- may cause rejection if unsigned

The letter must include:

- indication that the signer is authorized to sign the proposal and contract
- the signer’s title or position
- if the signer is not the contact person on the title page, the address and telephone number of the authorized signatory
- a statement that the proposer is willing to enter into a contractual agreement containing at least the terms and conditions in Attachment A
- any exceptions to those terms must be stated in a separate section of the proposal

### D. Executive Summary

The executive summary must be concise and no more than one page. It must summarize the key points, including which services in the Scope of Services are included in the proposal.

### E. Proposer’s Background and Experience

Must include:

1. History and background of the firm, including:
   - date organized
   - if incorporated, when and where incorporated
2. Firm profile including:
   - brief description of full range of services
   - total number of offices, addresses of each, and total number of employees
   - location of the office that will manage the engagement and number of personnel there
   - list of all former and current clients for the past 3 years
   - scope of services provided to each listed client
3. Name, address, telephone number, fax number, and website if applicable
4. Contact person(s), including email address(es)
5. Brief history of the firm, its healthcare experience, and its length of time in operation
6. List of principals to be used on the engagement, including project role
7. Copy of the firm’s most recent financial statement

### F. Experience

Must include:

1. Other projects of similar scope and complexity
2. References for at least three healthcare clients, including:
   - contact person name
   - address
   - telephone number
3. Specific healthcare experience, including description of the size and nature of the projects

### G. Staffing Proposal

Must include:

- list of staff who will participate in the project
- role of each person
- level of effort
- qualifications

Must also address:

1. justification of staffing proposal
2. customer service training, initial and ongoing
3. metrics used to ensure best-in-class service

### H. Fee Proposal

The fee proposal:

- must be submitted in the proposer’s package
- must be a separate, clearly marked document
- should include as much detail as possible on the components of compensation
- must include subtotals by task and phase of work

WMCHealth reserves the right to negotiate a lower or different fee structure with a tentatively selected proposer.

### I. Additional Information

The proposal should explain:

- why the firm should be selected
- what the firm can do for the Corporation that other firms cannot
- any other information that helps answer those questions

### J. Conflicts of Interest

Must include:

1. Statement of any financial interest of any employee, officer, director, or affiliated physician or physician practice of the Corporation in the proposer’s firm
2. Statement of any potential conflict of interest or appearance of impropriety involving other clients of the proposer’s firm or employees of the Corporation or New York Medical College
3. Whether any owner, officer, or employee of the proposer’s firm has served as an officer of, or been employed by, the Corporation or its affiliates during the prior 12 months
4. Procedures to detect, notify, and resolve conflicts of interest
5. Any pending litigation or regulatory action that could materially affect the firm’s ability to serve the Corporation
6. Whether the firm has ever:
   - had a government contract terminated for any reason
   - received any governmental or regulatory criminal or civil sanctions

### K. Attached Forms

All proposals must include completed copies of:

- Attachment C
- Attachment D
- Attachment E
- Attachment F
- Attachment G

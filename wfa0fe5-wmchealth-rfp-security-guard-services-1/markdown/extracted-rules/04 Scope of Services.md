# Scope of Services

Source: Section `4. Scope of Services` from the WMCHealth RFP markdown.

## Overall Scope

WMCHealth is seeking proposals from qualified firms to provide security guard services across the network.

The RFP notes that:

- WMCHealth currently has one incumbent contracted security vendor at Westchester Medical Center and MidHudson Hospital
- WMCHealth currently has one incumbent contracted security vendor at BSCHS
- HealthAlliance internally staffs its security personnel

The vendor is expected to consider current services and make recommendations regarding continuation or improvement.

## Personnel Duties and Responsibilities

The proposal should address these services:

- concierge services
- two security officers providing administrative services at Valhalla
- security operations/control center staffed 24/7
- CCTV, alarm, and radio monitoring
- assignments in Behavioral Health Center areas
- patient screening and safeguarding patient property
- visitor screening and weapons detection screening
- assisting clinicians who engage patients that may require restraint
- escort and transportation support for staff
- courier and shuttle services for GSH, BSCH, and SACH

The RFP also states:

- prevailing wage or other pay and benefit requirements apply at Westchester Medical Center and MidHudson Hospital
- this did not apply to Bon Secours Charity Health System or HealthAlliance at the time of issuance, but may change

## Training

The proposal should address:

- WMCHealth-approved de-escalation training, including initial and annual refresher training
- completion of all mandatory WMCHealth training

## Uniforms and Equipment

The proposal should address vendor responsibility for:

- uniforms at no additional cost to WMCHealth or officers
- 4x4 vehicles at no additional cost:
  - two for Valhalla
  - one for MHRH
  - one for HAHV
- officer equipment including cell phones, tablets, and other necessary electronics
- weapons, ammunition, and required training for armed officers at no additional cost
- replenishing or refurbishing depleted supplies used by security personnel
- sign-out accountability for equipment or supplies issued by WMCHealth
- vehicle patrol, transport, and shuttle responsibilities

## Armed Coverage Requirement

The proposal should account for `24/7 Armed Security Officer coverage` at:

- Valhalla
- MHRH
- HAHV
- Good Samaritan
- St. Anthony’s Community
- Bon Secour Community Hospitals

## Emergency Response Requirements

The proposal should address response requirements for:

- active shooter
- workplace violence response and investigation
- prisoner responsibilities or management requirements
- use of force or restraint requirements
- panic alarm response
- emergency codes response, including:
  - fire emergencies
  - HazMat incidents
  - infant or child missing or abducted
  - non-patient medical emergencies

## Administration

The proposal should reflect the following administrative expectations:

- post orders already exist
- personnel records must be kept on-site in a neat and orderly fashion for immediate inspection
- files must document:
  - current medical clearances
  - initial and annual training received
  - state licenses
  - other necessary certifications
- an electronic records management system is in place

## Insurance Requirements Mentioned in Scope

The RFP scope section identifies these minimum insurance categories:

- Worker’s Compensation and Disability compliance documents
- Employer’s Liability: `$1,000,000`
- General Liability: `$5,000,000`
- Firearms Liability: `$2,000,000`
- Professional Liability: `$2,000,000`
- Fidelity Bond / 3-D policy
- Automobile Liability: `$2,000,000`
- Cyber Liability: `$5 million per occurrence`
- Excess Liability / Umbrella Insurance

These insurance details should be checked against the full RFP and final legal or insurance review before submission.

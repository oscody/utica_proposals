# Maryland Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Security Guard Agency License
- Regulated by the Maryland State Police Licensing Division

## 2. Core Requirement

- A company providing security guard services in Maryland must be licensed as a Security Guard Agency before operating.
- Maryland is a qualifier state with real experience requirements.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- Applicant or firm representative must be at least 25 years old
- Must be of good character and reputation
- Must meet one of Maryland’s experience paths, including combinations such as:
  - 5 years as a full-time private detective
  - 5 years as a full-time Maryland police officer with approved police training
  - 3 years as a detective while serving as a police officer
  - 3 years in an investigative law-enforcement capacity with required police training
  - 5 years as a full-time fire investigator with approved training
  - 5 years as a full-time correctional supervisor at lieutenant rank or above with required training

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through the Maryland Licensing Portal
- If the applicant is a firm, each firm member must complete the required background process
- Maryland says completed applications are generally processed within 90 days

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Commercial general liability insurance is required
- If the agency employs 5 or more guards: $1,000,000 total aggregate minimum
- If the agency employs fewer than 5 guards: $500,000 total aggregate minimum
- Maryland’s statute says the policy must include errors and omissions and completed operations

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Initial individual (unincorporated): $200
- Initial firm (incorporated): $375
- Renewal individual: $200
- Renewal firm: $400
- MSP says fees are paid electronically in the Licensing Portal

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Each guard must hold Maryland security guard certification
- Initial guard certification fee: $15 plus fingerprint fees
- Renewal guard certification fee: $10 plus fingerprint fees
- Maryland requires 12 hours of initial security training unless exempt

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Make sure the agency qualifier meets Maryland’s experience rule
3. Obtain the required Maryland liability insurance
4. File the agency application in the MSP Licensing Portal
5. Certify each guard before assigning them

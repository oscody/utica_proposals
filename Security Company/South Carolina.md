# South Carolina Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Private Security Business License
- Regulated by the South Carolina Law Enforcement Division (SLED)

## 2. Core Requirement

- South Carolina requires a SLED business license to operate a private security business.
- Employee registration alone is not enough; the company itself must be licensed.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- The business license must be issued to a principal of the applicant business
- Principals must be of suitable character and background under South Carolina law
- SLED treats bodyguard and executive-protection work as private security, so the company license is still required

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through SLED Regulatory Services
- Business and trade names must be approved by SLED
- The words “security” or “protection” cannot be used in the business name unless the entity is properly licensed
- Advertising to the public must include the SLED license number

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- I found clear SLED / regulation support for licensing and fees, but not a clean official insurance figure in the sources reviewed for this batch
- This is one of the states I would verify again before relying on it for final filing paperwork

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Private Security Contract / Proprietary Business license: $350 annually
- License term: 1 year
- Renewal application must be received by SLED at least 30 days, but not more than 60 days, before expiration

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Armed security officer registration: $110 annually
- Unarmed security officer registration: $65 annually
- Replacement card: $20
- Employees must be registered through the licensed business

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Make sure the business principal is the SLED applicant
3. Get SLED approval of the business name and license
4. Pay the annual business-license fee
5. Register each armed or unarmed officer through the company

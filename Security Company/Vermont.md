# Vermont Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Private Security Guard Services Agency License
- Regulated by the Vermont Secretary of State, Office of Professional Regulation

## 2. Core Requirement

- Vermont licenses the agency itself if you want to operate a security services business.
- Vermont is simpler than many states because the statute for the agency license is mostly filing-based, while individual guards carry the experience and exam requirements.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- For the agency license itself, Vermont law requires:
  - proper application,
  - application fee, and
  - evidence of current Vermont business entity registration
- If someone will practice independently as a security guard rather than only through the agency hierarchy, Vermont requires at least 2 years of satisfactory security-work experience for that individual license

## 4. Business Requirements

- Submit the agency application through Vermont OPR
- Show current Vermont business entity registration
- If employing guards or investigators, the agency must register its employees with the Office
- The agency must maintain training records at its regular place of business within Vermont

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- I did not find a Vermont statute in this chapter imposing a general agency bond or insurance requirement for security agencies
- This should still be checked for contract-level or local requirements before operating

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Agency application fee: $390
- Agency biennial renewal: $345
- Vermont’s fee statute groups private investigator or security services agency licenses together

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Agency employees must be registered with OPR
- Employees must carry identification showing the employing licensee
- Armed guard work requires an active specialty designation authorizing firearms
- Vermont requires examination for licensed security guards, and independent security guard licensure requires at least 2 years of satisfactory experience

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Register the Vermont business entity
2. Apply for the agency license through OPR
3. Register every employee working under the agency
4. Make sure armed personnel have the needed specialty authorization
5. Keep required training records in Vermont

# Nevada Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Private Patrolman Agency License
- Regulated by the Nevada Private Investigators Licensing Board

## 2. Core Requirement

- Nevada requires a state agency license for a private patrol or contract security business.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- Nevada uses a qualified-agent structure for agency operations
- Background review and board approval are part of the process

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through PILB
- Branches and work cards are handled under Nevada’s private patrol system

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Confirm current Nevada bond and insurance requirements directly with PILB before filing

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Nevada uses board-set agency and work-card fees
- Confirm the current exact private patrol agency fee in the PILB packet

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Employees need Nevada work cards before they can work

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Set up the Nevada qualified-agent structure
3. Confirm current bond and agency fee with PILB
4. Apply for the private patrol agency license
5. Get work cards for employees

## 9. Sources

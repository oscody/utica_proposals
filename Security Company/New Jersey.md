# New Jersey Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Security Officer Company License
- Regulated by the New Jersey State Police Private Detective Unit under SORA

## 2. Core Requirement

- If you want to operate a contract security company in New Jersey, you need a Security Officer Company License.
- New Jersey is a true agency-license state with owner approval, company licensing, and employee certification.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- At least one owner or operator must have either:
  - 5 years of law enforcement experience and no longer be attached to a law enforcement agency, or
  - 5 years of supervisory or management experience with a licensed security officer company
- Owners / operators apply through the State Police process
- The state screens applicants and runs background checks

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through the New Jersey State Police Private Detective Unit
- Each business location needs its own company license
- Branches and additional locations require approval

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- $5,000 surety bond required

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Agency owner initial fee: $300
- Agency licensing fee: $500 per location
- Agency owner renewal: $220.62
- Agency renewal license: $500
- Branch license: $500
- License term: 2 years

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Security officers must be certified under SORA
- Security officer initial fee: $75
- Security officer renewal fee: $80.87
- New employee registration materials and compliance are handled through the Private Detective Unit / SORA process

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the business
2. Make sure one owner or operator meets the 5-year experience rule
3. Get the $5,000 surety bond
4. Apply for owner approval and company license through NJSP
5. Certify officers under SORA before assigning them

# Alaska Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Security Guard Agency License
- Regulated by the Alaska Department of Public Safety

## 2. Core Requirement

- Alaska requires a state agency license to operate a security guard company.
- The company must also have a qualified agent.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- Applicant and qualified agent must be at least 18
- Must be a U.S. citizen or resident alien with work permit
- No felony conviction within 10 years
- Must meet the training and qualification rules in Alaska regulations

## 4. Business Requirements

- Must hold a valid Alaska business license
- Changes in ownership or qualified agent require a new application
- Branch locations need duplicate license display

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Alaska requires the agency to meet insurance or bond requirements under 13 AAC 60.120

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Change of ownership fee: $200
- Change of qualified agent fee: $25
- Confirm current initial and renewal fees with Alaska DPS when filing

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Guards need Alaska security guard licenses
- Guard must complete preassignment training

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company and get the Alaska business license
2. Pick a qualified agent
3. Meet Alaska insurance or bond requirements
4. Apply for the agency license
5. License and train each guard

# Georgia Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Private Security Company License
- Regulated by the Georgia Board of Private Detective and Security Agencies

## 2. Core Requirement

- A company must be licensed before operating a private security business in Georgia.
- Georgia is a real agency-license state and requires the company applicant to pass the Georgia security exam.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- The person filing for a company license must be a corporate officer or company officer
- That person must meet Georgia’s general qualifications, including:
  - at least 18 years old
  - U.S. citizen or registered resident alien
  - good character, competency, and integrity
- Georgia’s Board FAQ states all applicants for company licensure must take the exam for Private Detective and/or Security Agency

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through the Georgia Secretary of State licensing system
- Choose the correct company type:
  - Private Security Company, or
  - Dual Private Detective / Security Company if doing both
- Georgia requires company licensure even if the business is already licensed somewhere else

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Georgia law requires either:
  - a surety bond,
  - liability insurance, or
  - a net-worth affidavit as allowed by statute
- Confirm the exact filing option and amount with the Board packet when applying

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Initial Private Security Company total fee: $600
- Renewal fee is listed on the Board fee schedule
- ID card fee: $50 if ordered

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Private security employees must be registered through the company
- Initial armed security employee registration fee: $70
- Company must apply for employee registration on the guard’s behalf
- Some active Georgia peace officers are exempt from separate employee licensure, but not from company licensure

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Have the company officer complete the Georgia company-licensure process
3. Pass the required Georgia security exam
4. Submit the required bond / insurance / net-worth filing
5. Register each employee through the company

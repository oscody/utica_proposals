# Alabama Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Contract Security Company License
- Regulated by the Alabama Security Regulatory Board

## 2. Core Requirement

- A company providing contract security services in Alabama needs a Contract Security Company license.
- Alabama requires a licensed Qualifying Agent tied to the company.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- The company must designate a Qualifying Agent
- The Qualifying Agent must be an employee of the company
- The Qualifying Agent must have either:
  - at least 3 years of experience as a manager, supervisor, or administrator with a contract security company, or
  - at least 3 years of supervisory experience with a federal, military, state, county, or municipal law-enforcement agency
- The Qualifying Agent cannot serve as Qualifying Agent for another Alabama contract security company

## 4. Business Requirements

- Submit the company application with Secretary of State / revenue registration materials
- Submit the Qualifying Agent’s personal-license materials with the company filing
- The company must keep its Alabama license and Board compliance current

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Certificate of insurance required
- Minimum coverage:
  - $2,000,000 for bodily or personal injury
  - $200,000 for property damage
- Policy must include general liability, personal injury, and workers’ compensation endorsements

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Company license fee: $250
- Qualifying Agent personal license fee: $112 under the Board’s current 2025 fee notice
- Company licenses expire September 30 each year
- Renewal application should be submitted before October 1

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Security officers must hold Alabama personal licenses
- Current personal application / license fee: $75 plus $37 background check = $112
- Alabama also regulates armed officers and trainers separately

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Pick a real Qualifying Agent with the required 3-year background
3. Obtain Alabama-compliant insurance
4. File the company and Qualifying Agent applications together
5. License each officer before assigning work

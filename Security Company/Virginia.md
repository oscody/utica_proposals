# Virginia Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Private Security Services Business License
- Regulated by the Virginia Department of Criminal Justice Services

## 2. Core Requirement

- A company cannot engage in or solicit private security services business in Virginia without a business license from DCJS.
- Virginia requires a designated compliance agent for the business.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- A business license cannot be issued until a compliance agent is designated in writing
- The compliance agent is responsible for keeping the business and its personnel compliant with Virginia law and regulations
- The compliance agent must meet DCJS qualification standards and hold compliance agent certification

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply to DCJS for the business license
- Select the service category or categories the business will provide
- The business cannot begin providing or soliciting services until the license is issued

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Proof of liability is required
- Virginia’s application materials require either:
  - a surety bond, or
  - a certificate of general liability insurance
- Minimum liability amount listed by DCJS application materials: $1,000,000

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Initial 1-year business license: $550
- Initial 2-year business license: $800
- 2-year business license renewal: $500
- Additional business category: $50
- Initial compliance agent certification: $50
- Compliance agent renewal: $25
- Fingerprint processing: $50

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Security officers must be registered with DCJS
- Initial registration: $25
- Registration renewal: $20
- Firearm endorsement: $10
- Fingerprint processing: $50

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Designate and certify a compliance agent
3. Obtain the required bond or liability insurance
4. Apply for the Virginia business license with the right service categories
5. Register each security officer with DCJS before deployment

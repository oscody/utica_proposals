# Connecticut Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Private Security Service License
- Regulated by the Connecticut Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection, Special Licensing and Firearms Unit

## 2. Core Requirement

- If you want to run a private security company in Connecticut, you need a company license first.
- Connecticut treats this as a real qualifier state, not an easy filing-only state.

## 3. Owner / Qualifier Requirements

- Applicant must be at least 25 years old
- Must have good moral character
- Must have either:
  - 5 years of supervisory security experience, or
  - 10 years as a police officer with a federal, state, or organized municipal police department
- If the applicant is a corporation, partnership, or association, the officer or member applying must meet those qualifications
- No license if the applicant is currently vested with police powers

## 4. Business Requirements

- File the business entity paperwork
- If using a corporation, submit the Connecticut certificate of incorporation
- If foreign, submit authority to do business in Connecticut
- Submit the application, photos, fingerprints, references, education proof, and other background materials required by SLFU

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- $10,000 performance surety bond required
- $300,000 general liability insurance policy required

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Individual initial license: $1,450
- Corporate / partnership / association initial license: $1,750
- Individual renewal: $625
- Corporate / partnership / association renewal: $1,000
- License term: 2 years

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Guards must get a Security Officer Certification Identification Card before the company registers them
- Guard must be at least 18
- Armed guard must be at least 21
- Employee registration fee: $40 per employee
- Guard card fee: $100 plus fingerprint/background charges listed by the state

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Make sure your qualifier meets Connecticut’s experience rule
3. Get the $10,000 bond and $300,000 liability policy
4. Apply for the Private Security Service License
5. Certify and register each guard before assigning them

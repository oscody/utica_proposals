# Pennsylvania Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Private Detective / Watch, Guard or Patrol Agency License
- Issued locally through the county court system under Pennsylvania’s Private Detective Act of 1953

## 2. Core Requirement

- Pennsylvania does require a license for a watch, guard, or patrol agency.
- This is not a simple statewide agency filing. It is a court-based licensing process.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- No license issued to a person under 25
- Applicant must be a U.S. citizen
- At least one individual applicant, or one member / officer of the business, must have at least 3 years of qualifying experience as:
  - a detective,
  - U.S. government investigative service member,
  - sheriff,
  - Pennsylvania State Police member, or
  - city police officer above patrolman rank
- Application must include approvals from 5 reputable citizens
- Felony and certain other criminal convictions are disqualifying

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through the court in the county where the office will be licensed
- The license covers the principal office and each bureau, agency, sub-agency, office, or branch office listed in the application
- Corporate applicants submit a certified copy of the certificate of incorporation

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- $10,000 corporate surety bond required

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Individual license fee: $200
- Partnership / association / corporation license fee: $300
- Original license lasts 2 years
- Renewal requires fee and bond again
- Renewal may be reissued for up to 5 years under the Act

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Employees must provide an employee statement before employment
- If carrying a lethal weapon, the employee must hold valid certification under the Lethal Weapons Training Act
- License holders cannot knowingly employ a person convicted of a felony or other listed disqualifying offenses

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Confirm one owner / officer meets the 3-year experience rule
3. Apply in the proper Pennsylvania county court
4. Post the $10,000 bond and pay the county license fee
5. Screen employees and handle armed-guard certification where needed

# Tennessee Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Contract Security Company License
- Regulated by Tennessee Private Protective Services

## 2. Core Requirement

- A person providing security guard and patrol service on a contractual basis in Tennessee must hold a Contract Security Company license.
- Tennessee allows qualification by experience or by passing the exam.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- Owner or Qualifying Agent must be:
  - at least 21 years old
  - a U.S. citizen or resident alien
  - legally and mentally competent
  - of good moral character
- Must satisfy either:
  - 3 years of experience as a manager, supervisor, or administrator with a contract security company or proprietary security organization, or
  - 3 years of supervisory experience with a public law-enforcement agency, including military, or
  - pass the required examination

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through Tennessee’s CORE system
- If a partnership, each partner must qualify
- If a corporation or LLC, an agent of the company must qualify
- Fingerprints are required

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Certificate of insurance required
- Minimum coverage:
  - $300,000 for bodily or personal injury
  - $100,000 for property damage
- Policy must include the Tennessee-required endorsements and notice language

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Initial application fee: $300
- Initial company license fee:
  - $125 for companies employing 0-49 guards
  - $425 for companies employing 50+ guards
- Renewal fee every 2 years:
  - $300 for companies employing 0-49 guards
  - $600 for companies employing 50+ guards
- License term: 2 years

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Unarmed guard application fee: $95
- Armed guard application fee: $130
- Unarmed guard renewal: $70
- Armed guard renewal: $85
- Tennessee separately regulates guard registration and training

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Make sure the owner or qualifying agent meets the 3-year experience rule or passes the exam
3. Obtain Tennessee-compliant insurance
4. File the Contract Security Company application through CORE
5. License each armed or unarmed guard separately

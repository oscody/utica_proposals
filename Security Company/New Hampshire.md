# New Hampshire Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Security Guard Service License
- Regulated by the New Hampshire Department of Safety

## 2. Core Requirement

- No person may engage in the business of a security guard agency or security guard service in New Hampshire without a license from the commissioner.
- Employees also need their own license before working for the agency.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- The application must identify the person primarily responsible for managing and conducting the business in New Hampshire
- The application must include 3 unrelated character references for an original license
- The statute requires strong integrity and honesty standards
- New Hampshire’s statute spells out experience qualifications for private investigator and bail recovery applicants, and the commissioner sets broader licensing standards by rule
- Applicants must disclose convictions, prior discipline, claims, and lawsuits

## 4. Business Requirements

- If applying from out of state as a corporation, register as a foreign corporation in New Hampshire
- The application must identify the firm, partners, officers, and major stockholders
- The licensee must report personnel, office, and address changes within 10 business days

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Surety bond required
- Minimum bond: $50,000
- Increases by $50,000 for each additional type of license held

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Agency / service license fee:
  - $150 if employing one licensed person
  - $350 if employing more than one licensed person
- License term: 2 years
- Renewal requires proof that the surety bond remains in force

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Employees of a security guard agency must be licensed before employment
- The commissioner sets the employee fee by rule, up to $100
- Armed guards must also meet New Hampshire firearms proficiency requirements

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Register in New Hampshire if you are an out-of-state corporation
3. Identify the manager responsible for in-state operations
4. Post the $50,000 surety bond
5. Get the agency license and then license each employee

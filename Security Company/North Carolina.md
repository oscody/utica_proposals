# North Carolina Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Security Guard and Patrol License
- Regulated by the North Carolina Private Protective Services Board

## 2. Core Requirement

- A person or firm providing contract security guard services in North Carolina must be licensed.
- The firm must employ at all times at least one license holder designated as the qualifying agent.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- The license is issued to a sole proprietor or individual in a management position with the firm
- General requirements:
  - at least 18 years old
  - high school diploma or equivalent
  - U.S. citizen or resident alien
  - good moral character
- For Security Guard and Patrol, the qualifier needs 3 years of experience as a manager, supervisor, or administrator with:
  - a contract security company,
  - a proprietary security organization, or
  - a law-enforcement agency performing a guard-and-patrol function

## 4. Business Requirements

- File the license application through the Board’s online system
- Submit the company business application if not a sole proprietorship
- North Carolina says the process typically takes 2 to 4 months

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Financial responsibility liability insurance certificate required for Security Guard and Patrol licensees
- North Carolina’s renewal materials specifically require the certificate for this license type

## 6. Fees & Validity

- License application fee: $188
- License issuance fee if approved: $550
- Renewal fee: $500
- Additional company fee if licensed with more than one company: $500
- License term: 2 years

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- New unarmed guard registration: $30 application fee + $38 fingerprint fee
- New armed guard registration: $45 application fee + $38 fingerprint fee
- Unarmed guard training: 16 hours
- Armed guard training: 16-hour unarmed course plus 20-hour armed course
- The company files registration for each guard

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Make sure the qualifying agent has the required 3 years of guard/patrol experience
3. File the board application and company materials
4. Maintain the required liability insurance
5. Register each guard through the company

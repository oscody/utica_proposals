# Massachusetts Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Watch Guard / Patrol Agency License
- Regulated through the Massachusetts State Police Certification Unit

## 2. Core Requirement

- A company providing watch, guard, or patrol services in Massachusetts needs this license.
- Massachusetts is more qualification-heavy than Florida, but still straightforward if you have a clean background and proper paperwork.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- Applicant must be of good moral character
- No felony disqualifier
- Investigative experience is not required for a Watch Guard license
- The applicant, corporation, resident manager, superintendent, or official representative cannot currently be POST-certified active law enforcement
- Application needs certifications from 3 reputable Massachusetts citizens who have known the applicant for 3 or more years

## 4. Business Requirements

- Submit the Private Investigator & Watch Guard License Form
- Submit through the Certification Licensing System
- Mail the notarized application packet
- Include Massachusetts business filing documents for the company
- Business name is subject to approval by the State Police

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Minimum $5,000 surety bond required

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Original application fee: $550
- Renewal fee: $150
- The state application page confirms the fees; verify renewal timing directly with the Certification Unit when filing

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Employees working under the licensed watch guard agency must submit the Massachusetts employee affidavit
- No person convicted of a felony or crime of moral turpitude may be employed by the licensee

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the Massachusetts entity
2. Prepare the watch guard application and references
3. Obtain the $5,000 surety bond
4. File online and mail the notarized packet
5. Have employees complete the required affidavit process

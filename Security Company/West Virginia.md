# West Virginia Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Security Guard Firm License
- Regulated by the West Virginia Secretary of State

## 2. Core Requirement

- You cannot operate a security guard business in West Virginia without a license from the Secretary of State.
- West Virginia uses a qualifying-agent structure.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- The qualifying agent must complete the security guard license application
- The qualifying agent must be directly involved with and responsible for supervising security guard operations
- West Virginia requires proof of the qualifying agent’s training and/or experience
- The official application guidance allows qualification through:
  - related degree or coursework,
  - qualifying licensed-firm experience,
  - equivalent law-enforcement employment, or
  - a combination of education and experience
- If qualification is based entirely on experience, the state guidance says it must include at least 32 hours per week for 100 weeks

## 4. Business Requirements

- File the firm application
- Each officer, member, or partner must submit the required materials
- West Virginia requires five character references for the qualifying agent and for each officer, member, or partner

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Choose one:
  - $5,000 surety bond, or
  - proof of liability insurance acceptable to the Secretary of State

## 6. Fees & Validity

- License fee: $250
- Includes a $50 nonrefundable processing fee
- License term: 2 years

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- The firm may not employ individuals to perform covered duties unless they comply with the statute and rules
- West Virginia law separately regulates security guards and firm employees under the same article

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Pick a real qualifying agent who will run security operations
3. Gather references and qualification documents
4. Get the $5,000 bond or acceptable liability insurance
5. Apply for the firm license with the Secretary of State

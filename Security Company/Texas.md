# Texas Security Company License

## 1. License Name

- Security Services Contractor Company License
- Regulated by Texas Department of Public Safety, Private Security Program

## 2. Core Requirement

- Texas requires a state company license for a contract security business.
- Texas also registers owners and managers tied to the license.

## 3. Owner / Qualification Requirements

- Owners, officers, partners, and some managers are registered under the Texas private security system
- Texas uses a company-license plus individual-registration structure

## 4. Business Requirements

- Apply through Texas DPS Private Security
- Branches and management roles are separately handled in the Texas system

## 5. Insurance / Bond

- Texas requires proof of liability insurance
- Texas has recently enforced this requirement actively, including license suspensions

## 6. Fees & Validity

- Owners, officers, partners, or shareholders: $55
- Noncommissioned security officer / manager / branch manager registration listed on DPS fee sheet
- Company fee should be confirmed from the current Texas company-license packet

## 7. Employee Guard Requirements

- Noncommissioned and commissioned guards are registered separately
- Armed work requires the commissioned-officer path

## 8. Simple Summary

1. Form the company
2. Apply for the Texas company license
3. Register the owners and managers
4. Maintain the required liability insurance
5. Register all officers separately
